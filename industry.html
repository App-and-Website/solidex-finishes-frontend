Durable & Hygienic Flooring Built for Food Production

In the demanding food and beverage manufacturing industry, flooring systems face constant exposure to heavy foot and machinery traffic, temperature fluctuations, chemical spills, and moisture. These challenging conditions can quickly degrade traditional flooring, leading to safety risks and hygiene issues. At SolidEx Finishes, we specialise in delivering epoxy flooring solutions that meet HACCP guidelines and Australian safety standards.

Our epoxy coatings create a non-porous, easy-to-clean surface that prevents dirt, bacteria, and contaminants from accumulating in cracks or joints. Designed for areas such as processing zones, packaging lines, and cold storage rooms, our floors offer superior resistance to impact, heat, and chemicals while maintaining a sleek, professional finish. We ensure your workspace remains safe, compliant, and visually attractive for years.

Common Flooring Challenges in Food & Beverage Facilities

Epoxy flooring systems are engineered to deliver exceptional performance, safety, and visual appeal for every environment. Designed with durability and practicality in mind, each feature provides lasting value — from superior strength and chemical resistance to easy maintenance and seamless finishes. Whether for industrial, commercial, or food-grade spaces, epoxy floors ensure reliable protection, long-term performance, and a professional finish.

Maintaining Hygiene

Food production areas require seamless, non-porous floors that prevent bacterial growth and are easy to disinfect daily.

Chemical & Moisture Exposure

Cleaning agents, acidic ingredients, and water spills can erode traditional flooring. Epoxy systems resist both moisture and harsh chemicals.

Slip & Safety Hazards

Wet environments increase accident risks. Our anti-slip epoxy coatings ensure superior traction for workers and equipment.

Thermal & Heavy Load Stress

Temperature fluctuations and heavy machinery can cause cracking or delamination — epoxy absorbs impact while maintaining flexibility.

Case Study: Beverage Plant, Western Sydney

This case study highlights how SolidEx transformed the flooring system of a major beverage manufacturing facility in Western Sydney. The goal was to deliver a hygienic, chemical-resistant, and durable epoxy flooring solution suitable for continuous production and frequent washdowns.

Client Challenge

The client, a busy beverage production plant, required a food-grade flooring system that could endure heavy forklift movement, thermal shocks from hot washdowns, and constant exposure to cleaning chemicals — all without interrupting daily operations. Maintaining hygiene and safety while minimizing downtime was the top priority.

Our Solution

SolidEx designed and installed a high-performance, food-grade epoxy coating system with a reinforced anti-slip texture and superior thermal shock resistance. Our expert team scheduled the project over a weekend to minimize disruption, completing the full surface preparation, coating, and curing process within 72 hours. The seamless, easy-to-clean finish met strict HACCP hygiene requirements.

Results & Impact

Within six months of installation, the beverage plant experienced measurable improvements:

  • 40% faster cleaning cycles due to the smooth, non-porous epoxy surface.
  • Zero slip incidents since installation, improving workplace safety and compliance.
  • Reduced maintenance costs and improved workflow efficiency across production zones.
  • Long-term durability, maintaining a fresh and hygienic appearance under heavy operational loads.

This project demonstrates SolidEx’s expertise in delivering tailored industrial epoxy flooring solutions that meet the highest standards of safety, hygiene, and performance even under demanding conditions.
